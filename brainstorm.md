**App Ideas**
1. AnonChate (Anonymous Chat App)
A fully anonymous, school‑community–based chat platform where users can post messages, react, and join topic channels without using personal identities.
2. Study Buddy Finder
An app that matches students with study partners based on classes, schedules, and preferred study methods.
3. MoodTracker Mini
A simple app for teens to log daily moods with emojis, track patterns over time, and view positivity streaks.
4. TaskSnap
A photo‑based reminder app where users take a quick picture and set a timer. When the timer rings, the image reminds them exactly what they needed to do.
5. Local Events Radar
An app that shows school events, clubs, and local teen‑friendly activities happening soon, with RSVP and reminders.
6. QuickChef Teens
A recipe helper for simple meals using ingredients already in the fridge—users enter what they have and get 3 quick recipe ideas.

**Top 3 Ideas**
Idea 1: AnonChate (Anonymous Chat App)
Mobile: Strong — works great on mobile for quick posting and browsing.
Story: Allows students to express themselves safely without pressure.
Market: Similar to Fizz/YikYak — high familiarity and interest.
Habit: High — users check updates frequently.
Scope: Manageable for MVP (signup, posting, viewing messages).

Idea 2: Study Buddy Finder
Mobile: Good — students check schedules often.
Story: Helps students feel supported academically.
Market: Useful for high school + college.
Habit: Medium — used more around exam weeks.
Scope: Moderate (matching system adds complexity).

Idea 3: MoodTracker Mini
Mobile: Perfect fit — quick daily check‑ins.
Story: Helps teens track trends and emotional patterns.
Market: Mental wellness apps are popular.
Habit: High — users may log once daily.
Scope: Simple MVP.

**Final App Idea Chosen**
Final Decision: AnonChat
Your group selected AnonChate, an anonymous school‑community chat app where users can post messages, browse feeds, and interact without exposing their identity.
